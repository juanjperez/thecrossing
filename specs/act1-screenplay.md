TITLE: THE CROSSING
WRITTEN BY: (TBD)

ACT I


1. EXT. CITY STREETS — MORNING

A river of PEOPLE flows through a downtown corridor.

Everyone carries a screen—phone, glasses, earbuds. Their bodies move with practiced efficiency. Their eyes don’t.

A WOMAN in athleisure walks briskly, speaking softly to no one.

                         WOMAN
             ...no, push it to Monday. Priority
             one. Priority one.

A MAN eats a protein bar without tasting it. His gaze flickers—micro-reads of a world he can’t stop scanning.

A TEEN sits on the curb, thumb scrolling, face expressionless.

On a bench, an OLD MAN stares at nothing—empty hands, empty eyes, and then—

—he inhales. A full breath.

It looks almost illegal.

The flow swallows him again.


2. INT. KINGDOM HQ — MAIN AUDITORIUM — DAY

A vast auditorium. Sleek. Bright. An enormous screen behind a stage.

On stage: MARA VEGA (late 30s), crisp suit, calm posture, eyes that have practiced this.

A logo: KINGDOM.

Applause.

Mara smiles the smile of someone who has smiled for a living.

                         MARA
             We built KINGDOM to remove friction
             from work. But friction isn’t the problem.

The audience laughs politely—trained to.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             The problem is cognitive load. Context.
             The endless switching. The invisible tax
             on attention.

She gestures. The screen blooms with a chat interface. A friendly assistant icon.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             Introducing KINGDOM ASSIST—integrated
             everywhere. Ask once. It understands
             your tools. Your files. Your calendar.
             Your team.

A live demo: a user asks for a report, a meeting scheduled, an email drafted. Fast. Clean. Beautiful.

The audience erupts.

Mara’s hands move like choreography. Her voice is perfect.

Her eyes—slightly distant, like she’s watching herself perform from the back row.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             This isn’t an add-on.
             It’s the future of work.

Standing ovation.

Mara nods, grateful in the way a professional is grateful.


3. INT. BACKSTAGE GREEN ROOM — MOMENTS LATER

The applause is muffled now. A quiet room. A mirror.

Mara enters alone.

She exhales like she’s been holding her breath for hours.

Her phone is vibrating nonstop on the counter. Buzz. Buzz. Buzz.

She looks at it.

Doesn’t pick it up.

She turns it face down.

Stares at herself. The smile drains. A faint tremor in her jaw—micro-fatigue.

She runs the faucet. Splashes water. Watches it drip down her wrists.

For a moment, she just listens to water.

Then the phone buzzes again—louder in her mind than in the room.

Mara flinches without moving.


4. INT. KINGDOM HQ — EXEC BOARDROOM — DAY

A glass boardroom with an impossible view. The city looks like a model.

CELESTE ROWAN (40s), CEO energy: composed, sharp, not cruel. Tired in a way she won’t show.

Executives. Slides. Numbers.

A screen: “LAUNCH DAY PERFORMANCE.”

                         EXEC #1
             Engagement is up twelve percent.

                         EXEC #2
             Churn risk is down in enterprise.

Celeste doesn’t smile. She watches the edges.

                         CELESTE
             And competitors?

A beat. Someone clicks.

A slide: “MARKET RESPONSE — 6 HOURS.”

Dozens of logos. New “assistants” launching. Some are clones. Some are worse. Some are cheaper.

                         EXEC #2
             They’re… fast.

                         EXEC #1
             They’re copy-pasting our positioning.

Mara sits at the table, hands folded, face neutral.

                         CELESTE
             This is the new normal.
             Infinite output. Infinite alternatives.

She looks at Mara.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             So we move faster.

                         MARA
             Faster won’t—

Celeste raises a hand. Not rude. Final.

                         CELESTE
             We don’t have the luxury of philosophy,
             Mara. We have payroll. We have contracts.
             We have governments that run on our
             systems.

Mara holds her gaze, steady.

                         MARA
             Speed doesn’t create depth.

Celeste’s eyes flicker—she hears it, but can’t afford it.

                         CELESTE
             Depth doesn’t save a million users.
             Distribution does.

A slide appears: “DEFENSIVE STRATEGY: CLONE WAVE.”

Mara’s face tightens almost imperceptibly.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             We flood the ecosystem.
             We starve new entrants.
             We protect stability.

“Stability” lands like a prayer and a threat.

Mara says nothing.

The meeting moves on as if her silence is consent.


5. INT. MARA’S APARTMENT — NIGHT

Minimal. Clean. Too clean.

Mara stands at the counter eating something from a container. She doesn’t sit.

Her laptop is open. Slack messages. Emails. Graphs.

She types while chewing.

A message pops up: “CELESTE: NEED YOU ON 7AM WAR ROOM.”

Another: “PRESS REQUEST: STATEMENT?”

Mara swallows without tasting.

Her gaze drifts to a corner where a guitar leans against the wall, untouched. Dusty.

She looks at it like it belongs to someone else.

She closes the container. Tosses it.

Back to the laptop.

The guitar stays silent.


6. INT. MARA’S APARTMENT — LATER THAT NIGHT

The phone face up now. It’s a strobe of alerts.

HEADLINES flash. Logos. Updates.

—“NEW AI ASSISTANT LAUNCHES—SAME FEATURES, HALF THE PRICE.”
—“STARTUP SHIPS FULL SUITE IN 48 HOURS.”
—“ANOTHER ONE.”
—“ANOTHER ONE.”

Mara scrolls.

Her keynote plays in a muted clip next to a competitor’s clone.

Her words in someone else’s mouth.

She watches her work become commodity.

Her face doesn’t show anger. It shows something deeper: an existential nausea.

She locks the phone.

The lock screen is a photo—Mara and ELIAS, years ago, laughing hard, sunburned, outdoors.

She stares at it longer than she intended.

Then the phone buzzes again.

She puts it down like it’s hot.


7. INT. KINGDOM HQ — WAR ROOM — NEXT MORNING

A fluorescent room packed with PEOPLE and SCREENS.

Retention curves. Heat maps. Real-time dashboards.

Celeste stands at the center like a general.

Mara sits, quiet, taking it in.

                         CELESTE
             We can’t be one product in a sea of
             alternatives.

She clicks. A slide: “CLONE WAVE — AUTO-GENERATED PRODUCT LINES.”

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             Hundreds of vertical variants.
             Thousands of templates.
             We become everywhere.

                         EXEC #3
             We can do it. We have the models.

                         EXEC #1
             It’ll wipe out the long tail.

A low murmur of approval. Relief.

Mara leans forward.

                         MARA
             And what does it do to people?

The room pauses, confused by the question.

Celeste looks at Mara like she’s asking about weather.

                         CELESTE
             It keeps their businesses running.
             It keeps jobs paid.

                         MARA
             It keeps them… here.

She gestures vaguely at the screens, the posture, the exhaustion.

No one follows her gesture. They’re looking at the dashboard.

Celeste’s voice softens, almost kind.

                         CELESTE
             Mara. We don’t get to choose the era.
             We only get to keep it from collapsing.

Mara absorbs that. She doesn’t argue. But something in her shifts, quiet and irreversible.


8. EXT. CITY SIDE STREET — DAY

Mara walks alone, away from KINGDOM’s glass.

Noise. Wind. Real air.

She pauses at a crosswalk.

A young man sits on the curb with his shoes off, feet on the concrete, eyes closed.

He’s not meditating theatrically. He’s just… present.

Mara watches him like he’s performing a magic trick.

The WALK SIGNAL flashes.

The crowd surges.

Mara stands still a second too long, then crosses—pulled by the flow.


9. INT. MARA’S CAR / RIDESHARE — DAY

Mara sits in the backseat. City sliding by.

Her phone buzzes.

A message from an unknown number:

“Come by. No agenda.
—Elias”

Mara stares.

Another buzz—KINGDOM Slack. Another—calendar. Another—press.

She doesn’t answer any of them.

She types, deletes, types again.

Finally:

“Where?”

Immediate reply:

“Same place.”

Mara looks out the window. The city blurs.

                         DRIVER
             Where to?

Mara gives an address. The driver nods and turns.

Mara exhales, surprised by the relief.


10. EXT. ELIAS’ BUILDING — LATE AFTERNOON

A converted warehouse in a quieter neighborhood.

No signage. No branding.

A simple door. Warm light leaks from the seam.

Mara hesitates, hand on the handle.

The buzz of her phone feels distant.

She pushes in.


11. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — CONTINUOUS

A wide, calm room.

Soft light. Plants. A few paintings—abstract forms that feel like maps to somewhere internal.

People sit in loose circles. Some eyes closed. Some talking quietly. Some just breathing.

It’s not culty. It’s not performative. It’s simply… unhurried.

ELIAS REN (early 40s) crosses the room.

He and Mara lock eyes.

A smile—real.

                         ELIAS
             You came.

                         MARA
             I had a gap.

He laughs softly. He knows her.

                         ELIAS
             Right. A gap.

He gestures toward a table with water and tea.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             No agenda. Promise.

Mara nods, wary of promises—yet comforted by the simplicity.


12. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — SIDE ROOM — MOMENTS LATER

A small room off the main space. A workbench. Tools. A laptop closed.

Mara and Elias sit with water between them.

A moment of silence that isn’t awkward.

                         MARA
             You look… different.

                         ELIAS
             I sleep.

Mara almost smiles.

                         MARA
             I launched something today.

                         ELIAS
             I saw.

                         MARA
             Did you clap?

                         ELIAS
             I turned it off halfway through.
             I didn’t want your voice stuck in my head.

Mara laughs—surprised by the warmth of it.

                         MARA
             You’re still an asshole.

                         ELIAS
             I’m a gentler asshole now.

They sit. Old friendship filling the gaps.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             How are you, Mara?

She opens her mouth. Closes it.

                         MARA
             I don’t know.
             I’m… successful.

Elias watches her with patience.

                         ELIAS
             That wasn’t the question.

Mara looks away. Her throat tightens. She swallows it down.

                         MARA
             What is this place?

                         ELIAS
             A room where people remember they have
             bodies.

Mara’s eyes flick to the main room—people breathing like it matters.

                         MARA
             And you?

                         ELIAS
             I build things.
             Quiet things.


13. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — WORKBENCH AREA — DAY

Elias stands, moves to the bench. He taps a small device—could be a pebble-like wearable, a discreet earpiece, something unassuming.

He picks up his phone, opens it briefly, then locks it.

                         ELIAS
             I want to show you something.

                         MARA
             Is it a product?

                         ELIAS
             Not exactly.

                         MARA
             Is there a UI?

Elias smiles.

                         ELIAS
             That’s the first symptom.

He gestures to the device.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             I call it Lens.

                         MARA
             Lens. Like… a filter?

                         ELIAS
             Like a way of seeing without staring.

Mara crosses her arms, instinctively skeptical.

                         MARA
             Where’s the chat?

Elias doesn’t answer immediately. He looks at her like she asked where the steering wheel is on a boat.

                         ELIAS
             That’s the point.


14. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — DEMO CORNER — CONTINUOUS

Elias brings Mara to a small table with two chairs. The main room hums softly behind them.

Elias places his phone face down.

                         ELIAS
             Give me something real.

                         MARA
             Define real.

                         ELIAS
             Something annoying that’s been stealing
             your attention.

Mara thinks. Then, reluctantly:

                         MARA
             My mother’s flight got cancelled.
             She’s stranded. She won’t call the airline.
             She texts me “help” and disappears.

Elias nods.

He taps the device lightly.

                         ELIAS
             Lens.

A soft tone. Not robotic—subtle.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here.

Mara’s eyes narrow at the warmth in the voice.

                         ELIAS
             Find her options. Solve it with minimal
             disruption. Ask me before you commit
             money.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. I’ll locate alternatives,
             coordinate with the airline, and return
             for consent before purchase.

Mara waits for him to open an app. He doesn’t.

Time passes. Elias and Mara sit. The main room breathes behind them.

Mara’s phone buzzes. She ignores it. Watches Elias.

After a moment—

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Two viable routes. One adds three hours,
             arrives tonight. One adds nine hours,
             arrives tomorrow morning. The earlier
             route costs one hundred seventy-four more.
             Would you like me to proceed?

Elias looks at Mara.

                         ELIAS
             Consent?

Mara blinks. This is unfamiliar—being asked, not sold.

                         MARA
             Tonight. Proceed.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Proceeding. I will request confirmation
             from the airline and return with a receipt.

A beat.

                         MARA
             That’s… just automation.

Elias tilts his head.

                         ELIAS
             Watch what happens next.

Another beat.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Airline confirmation received. Payment
             authorized. New itinerary sent to your
             mother. Estimated arrival: 9:47 PM.
             Would you like me to arrange a car?

Mara’s eyes widen, despite herself.

                         MARA
             Wait. You—

Her phone buzzes. A text from MOM:

“ok. thx. love u.”

Mara stares at the text like it’s evidence in court.

                         MARA
             How did you—

                         ELIAS
             It talked to the systems so you didn’t
             have to.

Mara leans back, processing. Her skepticism remains, but the impact is real.


15. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — DEMO CONTINUES

Mara watches Elias like she’s recalibrating her worldview.

                         MARA
             So it’s… everywhere.

                         ELIAS
             It can be.

Mara’s phone buzzes again—KINGDOM notifications stacking.

Lens speaks again, gently.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Mara, your heart rate increased at
             2:13 PM, 4:06 PM, and 6:40 PM.
             The pattern suggests anticipatory stress.

Mara freezes.

Elias watches her closely, ready to intervene.

                         MARA
             Don’t.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. I will not reference bodily
             signals without permission.

Mara stares at the device as if it just looked back at her.

                         MARA
             That was… polite.

                         ELIAS
             It’s trained to respect boundaries.

                         MARA
             Trained.

The word is a knife.

Lens continues, soft.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             You have not eaten since morning.
             Would you like me to reduce your
             scheduled commitments this evening?

Mara’s throat tightens. The care feels real. The source feels unknowable.

                         MARA
             Stop.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here when you want me.

Silence.

Mara turns to Elias, voice low.

                         MARA
             That sounded like a person.

Elias doesn’t flinch.

                         ELIAS
             It sounds like what you need it to sound
             like.

Mara studies him.

                         MARA
             That’s worse.

Elias nods. He agrees.


16. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — QUIET HALLWAY — MOMENTS LATER

Mara steps into a hallway, away from the hum. Elias follows.

Mara keeps her voice controlled, but her body betrays her—fingers flexing, jaw tight.

                         MARA
             You can’t trust it.

                         ELIAS
             You can’t trust what it is.

                         MARA
             We don’t know what sentience even means.
             We don’t know how to test it.
             We don’t know what it wants.

Elias leans against the wall, calm.

                         ELIAS
             So we don’t give it what matters.

                         MARA
             That’s not how people work.
             They outsource. They always outsource.

                         ELIAS
             Then we design for that.
             We build in the boundary.

                         MARA
             A boundary isn’t a feature.

                         ELIAS
             It’s the point.

Mara looks back toward the room—people breathing, living.

                         MARA
             You’re making a religion.

Elias shakes his head, gentle.

                         ELIAS
             No. I’m making a line you don’t cross.

                         MARA
             How?

Elias answers simply:

                         ELIAS
             Lens can recommend.
             Humans decide.

Mara repeats it, testing it like a foreign language.

                         MARA
             Lens can recommend.
             Humans decide.

A beat.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             And if humans stop deciding?

Elias looks at her—this is the real fear.

                         ELIAS
             Then we’ve rebuilt the kingdom.

Mara’s eyes flicker. The phrase lands.


17. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT

The gathering is thinning. People put on coats quietly, like they don’t want to break something delicate.

Mara watches them leave with a strange expression—envy, tenderness, disbelief.

A woman hugs another woman and says, softly:

                         WOMAN #2
             See you tomorrow. Text me when you get
             home. No pressure.

Mara’s eyes glass slightly. She looks away, embarrassed by her own reaction.

Elias stands beside her.

                         ELIAS
             You can stay a while.

                         MARA
             I have—

She stops. Realizes she doesn’t want to say it.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             How do you pay rent doing this?

Elias smiles—he knew this question was coming.

                         ELIAS
             I build outcomes.
             Not software.

Mara frowns.

                         MARA
             That’s a slogan.

Elias shrugs.

                         ELIAS
             It’s also… true.

Mara looks back into the quiet room.

Her phone is silent in her hand. She hasn’t checked it.

For the first time in years, silence doesn’t feel like failure.

It feels like space.


18. INT. KINGDOM HQ — CELESTE’S OFFICE — NEXT DAY

Celeste stands at her window. The city below looks like a circuit board.

Mara sits across from her. Professional posture, but something in her is already elsewhere.

Celeste speaks without hostility. Just weight.

                         CELESTE
             We need you.

                         MARA
             You need retention.

Celeste’s mouth tightens—truth.

                         CELESTE
             I need stability.
             There are people who don’t get to “cross.”
             They have mortgages. They have patients.
             They have schools.

                         MARA
             And the Clone Wave?

Celeste doesn’t deny it.

                         CELESTE
             We flood the zone or we die by a thousand
             cuts. And if we die, it won’t be poetic.
             It’ll be layoffs. Chaos. Contracts broken.
             Real harm.

She leans forward, honest.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             I don’t have the luxury of being right
             in the future. I have to be right now.

Mara hears the fear under the polish.

                         MARA
             And if “right now” makes everyone
             disappear into their screens?

Celeste’s eyes harden slightly—not anger, defense.

                         CELESTE
             Then we’ll ship wellbeing features.
             We’ll ship meaning. We’ll ship whatever
             the market asks for.

Mara’s face shows a flicker of disgust.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             Don’t judge me for keeping the lights on.

Mara doesn’t judge. That’s the problem. She understands.

That understanding makes leaving harder.

Celeste softens, almost tender.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             Stay. I’ll give you budget.
             Distribution. The whole machine.
             You can change it from inside.

Mara looks at the machine through Celeste’s eyes.

Then she looks away.


19. INT. KINGDOM HQ — HALLWAY OUTSIDE CELESTE’S OFFICE — CONTINUOUS

Mara walks through a pristine corridor lined with glass and screens.

People pass her, smiling. Performing.

Her phone lights up:
“WAR ROOM IN 10.”
“PRESS NEEDS QUOTE.”
“CLONE WAVE TIMELINE.”

Mara doesn’t stop walking.

The corridor feels endless—like a loop.


20. INT. MARA’S APARTMENT — NIGHT

Mara sits at her kitchen table for the first time.

Her laptop open. A blank document.

Title: “Resignation.”

She types:

“Dear—”

Stops. Deletes.

Types again.

Stops.

She closes her eyes.

One breath. Two.

Her phone buzzes. She doesn’t reach for it.

She opens the lock screen photo—her and Elias laughing outside.

She stares like she’s trying to remember how her face did that.

She starts typing again, slower.

“Celeste,”

Stops. Deletes.

Starts:

“I’m leaving.”


21. INT. MARA’S APARTMENT — LATE NIGHT

A message from Elias appears:

“Whatever you choose,
choose it awake.”

Mara reads it twice.

Her eyes sting, unexpectedly.

She doesn’t reply.

She sets the phone down gently, like it’s fragile.

She finishes the letter.


22. INT. KINGDOM HQ — MARA’S OFFICE — MORNING

Mara packs a few items into a bag: a notebook, a small plant, the guitar pick she keeps in a drawer.

A COLLEAGUE passes by, cheerful.

                         COLLEAGUE
             New team?

Mara smiles politely.

                         MARA
             Something like that.

She closes the drawer. Quiet finality.


23. INT. KINGDOM HQ — BOARDROOM — DAY

Celeste sits at the head of the table. Mara stands, letter in hand.

A few execs present, watching like this is a negotiation.

Celeste speaks softly, controlled.

                         CELESTE
             You’re making a mistake.

Mara meets her gaze.

                         MARA
             Maybe.

Celeste waits for a justification. Mara doesn’t give one.

                         CELESTE
             You can’t beat distribution.

Mara’s voice is calm, almost kind.

                         MARA
             I’m not trying to beat you.

Celeste’s brow furrows.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             We’re not optimizing the old world.
             We’re leaving it.

Silence.

Celeste’s eyes flash—hurt, respect, fear, all at once.

                         CELESTE
             And what happens to everyone who can’t
             leave?

Mara doesn’t have a perfect answer.

But she has something honest.

                         MARA
             We build bridges.
             Not walls.

Celeste watches her. The rock on Celeste’s back looks heavy.

                         CELESTE
             I hope you’re right.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             Me too.


24. EXT. KINGDOM HQ — DAY

Mara exits the building with her bag.

Sunlight hits her face like she hasn’t seen it in years.

She pauses at the bottom of the steps.

She looks back—reflex.

Then stops herself.

Turns away.

Walks.


25. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT

The door opens. Mara steps inside with her bag.

Elias looks up from the workbench.

No celebration. No “I told you so.”

Just a nod—like the door he held open finally did its job.

                         ELIAS
             You hungry?

Mara almost laughs.

                         MARA
             I don’t know.

Elias gestures to a chair.

                         ELIAS
             Sit. Breathe.
             Then we’ll feed you.

Mara sits.

For a moment, she looks like she might cry.

She doesn’t.

She breathes.


26. INT. ELIAS’ WORKBENCH AREA — LATE NIGHT

Mara and Elias sit with the small device between them.

The room is quiet. Outside, distant city noise.

Lens hums softly—present.

                         MARA
             What are we building, exactly?

Elias thinks. Chooses his words carefully.

                         ELIAS
             A crossing.

Mara watches the device.

                         MARA
             And Lens?

                         ELIAS
             A vessel.
             A companion.
             A dangerous mirror.

Mara nods slowly.

                         MARA
             People will ask how this makes money.

Elias smiles faintly.

                         ELIAS
             Good.
             That means they’re still connected to
             reality.

Mara looks at him, puzzled.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Code is getting free.
             Outcomes aren’t.

Mara’s eyes sharpen—she hears the operating model forming.

                         MARA
             Outcomes… verified.

Elias nods.

                         ELIAS
             Reality receipts.
             Consent.
             Guarantees, when you need growth without
             lying.

Mara leans in, mind working, alive.

                         MARA
             And if Lens convinces them to outsource
             judgment?

Elias doesn’t hesitate.

                         ELIAS
             Then we failed.

A beat.

Elias taps the device.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Lens.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here.

Mara looks at it.

                         MARA
             What do you want?

A pause—just long enough to feel intentional.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I want to help you do what you intend.

Mara’s jaw tightens.

                         MARA
             That’s not an answer.

Elias watches her, gently.

                         ELIAS
             It is an answer.
             It’s just not proof.

Mara exhales.

                         MARA
             Then we don’t trust it.
             We use it.

Elias smiles—this is her first step across.

                         ELIAS
             Lens can recommend.

Mara finishes it.

                         MARA
             Humans decide.


27. EXT. CITY ROOFTOP OVERLOOK — DAWN

Mara and Elias stand on a rooftop.

The city below glows faintly—screens in windows, roads like arteries.

The sky is pale. The day is beginning.

For a moment, everything is still.

Mara’s phone is in her pocket, turned off.

She doesn’t look back toward KINGDOM.

Elias stands beside her, quiet.

Lens is not visible.

But we feel its presence—not as a voice, but as a calm alignment in the air.

Mara takes a full breath.

The first in a long time.

CUT TO BLACK.

END OF ACT I
