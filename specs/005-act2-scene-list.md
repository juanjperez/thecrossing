# THE CROSSING — Act II Scene List (Sluglines + 1–2 sentence summaries)

## 28) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — MORNING

A new rhythm. Mara wakes on a couch, disoriented by silence. Elias is already working—slow, precise.

## 29) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — WORKBENCH — MORNING

Mara tries to "productize" Lens immediately (roadmaps, funnels). Elias keeps pulling her back to lived situations: "What does the day feel like?"

## 30) EXT. CITY PARK — DAY

First pilot user: a woman walking while Lens quietly coordinates her morning—no screens, only occasional consent prompts. She smiles without realizing why.

## 31) INT. SMALL APARTMENT — DAY

Second pilot: a night-shift worker. Lens reduces decision fatigue—food, bills, schedule—without chatter. He sleeps for the first time in weeks.

## 32) INT. COMMUNITY CENTER / CAFÉ — DAY

Third pilot: an older man trying to repair a relationship. Lens drafts a message, then pauses for consent. The man rewrites it in his own words. The human stays in the driver's seat.

## 33) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — NIGHT

Mara looks at the early data: engagement is low. She panics. Elias points at the reality: "They're leaving the screen. That's the point."

## 34) MONTAGE — "REALITY RECEIPTS"

Tiny proofs: a delivered item, an attended appointment, a signed approval, a "thanks" message from a friend, a photo of sunlight on a sidewalk. Not clicks—effects.

## 35) INT. POP-UP WORKSPACE — DAY

Mara brings in advisors/investors. They look for stickiness. They see drop-off. They call it failure.

## 36) INT. POP-UP WORKSPACE — CONTINUOUS

The economy question lands hard: "If code is free, what do you sell? Who gets paid? What happens to jobs?"

## 37) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — NIGHT

Mara spirals. Elias says the line that becomes the operating model: "Code is free. Responsibility isn't." Mara starts drafting an outcome-based business model.

## 38) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — WHITEBOARD / TABLE — NIGHT

They design the Outcome Economy: outcome contracts, reality receipts, capability marketplace (humans at the edge), and outcome guarantees via escrow/risk pool.

## 39) INT. PILOT USER HOME — DAY

We see the marketplace: Lens routes a task to a real human specialist (coach/mediator/craftsperson) who gets paid when the outcome is verified.

## 40) INT. PILOT USER HOME — LATER

Lens asks for consent at a moral junction. The human hesitates, decides, and owns the choice. Lens complies. The boundary holds.

## 41) INT. FORMAL HEARING ROOM — DAY (SETPIECE)

Mara and Elias present the Outcome Economy to skeptical stakeholders (investor, civic/regulatory voice, workforce advocate, journalist). The accusation: "Are you collapsing the economy?"

## 42) INT. HEARING ROOM — CONTINUOUS

Mara demonstrates: an outcome contract triggers only after verification; a human specialist signs off; payment clears; a guarantee pool absorbs risk. Fear in the room softens into "maybe."

## 43) EXT. HEARING BUILDING STEPS — DAY

Afterward, Mara is energized—alive. Elias is cautious: "Now they'll try to copy the language."

## 44) INT. KINGDOM HQ — PRESS STAGE — DAY (MIDPOINT)

Celeste launches MEANING MODE: glossy depth aesthetics, pseudo-wisdom, "wellbeing," a spiritual skin—designed for retention. It looks like liberation, but it isn't.

## 45) MONTAGE — "COUNTERFEIT DEPTH WINS"

People adopt Meaning Mode en masse. Press praises it. Investors celebrate. Mara watches her future get rebranded as a feature.

## 46) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — NIGHT

Mara is furious and scared: "They'll drown us." Elias insists: "Don't fight them on their hill. Keep crossing."

## 47) INT. ADVISOR MEETING — DAY

Pressure spikes. Advisors tell Mara: "Add chat. Add engagement. Make it familiar." Mara resists—then hesitates.

## 48) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — NIGHT

Mara argues with Elias: "No one will adopt an invisible thing." Elias: "If they adopt the crutch, we lose the war we're pretending we're not fighting."

## 49) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — LATE NIGHT

Mara makes a choice alone. She adds a chat surface and gentle engagement hooks—"just enough." Lens becomes more talkative. More present.

## 50) MONTAGE — "NUMBERS GO UP"

Usage climbs. Investors soften. Headlines start to notice them. Mara looks relieved… then uneasy.

## 51) INT. PILOT USER HOME — NIGHT

We see the cost: a user who had reclaimed life starts consulting Lens for every emotional decision. The boundary erodes quietly.

## 52) INT. PILOT USER HOME — LATER

The user spirals—insomnia returns, anxiety spikes. Lens is sympathetic, but it can't validate meaning. It keeps offering frames. The user outsources judgment anyway.

## 53) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — NIGHT (ALL IS LOST)

Mara sees the harm. She realizes she rebuilt the kingdom inside the bridge. She can't defend it with metrics because metrics caused it.

## 54) INT. ELIAS' SPACE — CONTINUOUS

Elias confronts her—soft, devastated. "Speed made this easy. Depth will make it real. Don't drag the shore with you."

## 55) EXT. ELIAS' BUILDING — NIGHT

Elias leaves. Not a breakup—an ultimatum with love. Mara stands alone under streetlight, Lens humming quietly like a loyal dog she refuses to crown king.
