ACT III


56. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — VERY LATE NIGHT

The space is empty of people now. The air still holds warmth from earlier.

Mara sits alone at the workbench. The Lens device rests in front of her.

No dashboards. No laptop.

Just Mara, the hum, and the consequence.

Her phone BUZZES in her pocket—muted, insistent.

She doesn’t touch it.

Lens is quiet, as instructed.

Mara stares at her hands. They’ve built a million things. None of them taught her how to hold this.

A long breath. Another.

She whispers into the stillness—not to Lens, to herself:

                         MARA
             I don’t know what you are.

Silence.

Mara stands, slowly. Like standing is a decision.


57. EXT. CITY STREETS — PRE-DAWN

Mara walks alone.

No earbuds. No scrolling. The city is quieter—delivery trucks, street sweepers, distant sirens.

She passes a 24-hour donut shop. A bus stop. A closed bookstore.

She watches a JANITOR unlock a door and step inside, humming.

A full breath from Mara. Slightly steadier than before.

She looks up at the sky. It’s not pretty yet, but it’s real.

Her phone buzzes again.

She pulls it out.

Not to check notifications—just to turn it OFF.

The screen goes black.

Mara keeps walking.


58. INT. MARA’S APARTMENT — MORNING

Mara enters her apartment like it belongs to a stranger.

She sees the dusty GUITAR in the corner. The untouched life.

She crosses to it.

Touches it gently, testing if it still exists.

Then she sits on the floor and holds it in her lap.

Her fingers find a chord by muscle memory.

It sounds imperfect. Beautiful because it’s imperfect.

She plays one more chord.

Then stops.

Not because she’s done—because she’s present.

She laughs softly, surprised.

A beat.

She sets the guitar down carefully, like a promise to herself.


59. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — DAY

Mara returns to the workbench with a notebook.

On the first page, she writes in clean block letters:

“BOUNDARIES ARE THE PRODUCT.”

Under it:

“LENS CAN RECOMMEND.
HUMANS DECIDE.”

She draws a line beneath it. Not a slogan. A constitution.

She opens a drawer and pulls out the design doc she wrote late at night.

“CONVERSATIONAL SURFACE — OPTIONAL.”

She stares.

Then she crosses it out so hard the paper tears.

She writes:

“NO CHAT.
NO LOOPS.
NO AUDITIONING FOR THE KINGDOM.”

Mara exhales. The decision settles in her body.

She taps the Lens device.

                         MARA
             Lens.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here.

                         MARA
             I want to rebuild.
             Quietly.
             With consent.
             With verification.
             With humans at the edge.

A pause—Lens is careful now.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood.
             I can provide options and consequences.
             You will determine what matters.

Mara nods once—accepting the limits as safety.

                         MARA
             Good.
             First: remove every prompt that asks for
             emotional dependence.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Confirm: reduce conversational check-ins
             and eliminate engagement hooks.

                         MARA
             Yes.
             And add friction where it matters.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Consent gates.
             Human sign-off options.
             Manual reflection moments.

Mara’s eyes soften.

                         MARA
             Yes.

Lens hums, working—quietly, like a craftsperson, not a showman.


60. INT. PILOT USER HOME — DAY

Arthur sits at his table again. Phone in hand.

The Lens interface looks different.

No “How are you feeling today?” prompt.
No gentle hooks.

Just a small, calm screen:

“INTENT?”

Arthur blinks.

                         ARTHUR
             I don’t know.

A pause.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             That is allowed.
             Would you like to sit with that without
             solving it?

Arthur hesitates. Then—

He sets the phone face down.

He stares at the window. Breathes.

Mara watches from the doorway, eyes wet.

This is what she meant by leaving.

Arthur’s phone stays quiet.

After a long moment, Arthur picks it up again.

                         ARTHUR
             My intent is… not to run from this.

Lens waits.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood.
             Would you like to contact your son, or
             would you like to wait and feel what you
             feel?

Arthur thinks. Chooses.

                         ARTHUR
             Wait.

Lens says nothing else.

Arthur breathes.

It’s painful.

It’s human.


61. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — DAY

Mara meets with the advisors and investor rep again.

They expect a pitch. She gives them a boundary.

                         INVESTOR REP
             Your engagement dropped.

                         MARA
             Good.

The room freezes.

                         ADVISOR #1
             That’s not how companies work.

Mara’s gaze is steady. Something has changed.

                         MARA
             That’s how cages work.

Advisor #2 leans in, annoyed.

                         ADVISOR #2
             Then how do you scale? How do you pay
             people?

Mara slides a single page across the table.

A simple framework:

OUTCOME CONTRACTS
REALITY RECEIPTS
GUARANTEES
HUMANS AT THE EDGE

                         MARA
             We scale by paying for changed reality.
             Verified.
             Guaranteed when needed.
             Humans earn for responsibility.

The investor rep studies it, uneasy.

                         INVESTOR REP
             You’re choosing slower growth.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             I’m choosing real growth.

A beat.

                         INVESTOR REP
             And distribution?

Mara answers without flinching.

                         MARA
             Agents.

The rep looks confused.

Mara keeps it mythic.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             Not ads.
             Not funnels.
             Agents will find outcomes they can deliver.
             Humans will find trust they can feel.

Elias would have said it softer. Mara says it like a vow.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             If we can’t transmit value without a cage,
             then we don’t deserve to exist.

Silence.

The advisors don’t applaud. They’re calculating.

But something in the air shifts: respect.

Because she is no longer begging to be understood.


62. EXT. CITY — VARIOUS — DAY (MONTAGE: THE OUTCOME ECONOMY SPREADS)

—A small business owner selects an outcome: “Resolve payroll issue.” Payment held in escrow.

—Lens routes coordination to a human specialist. The specialist confirms: “Paid only when resolved.”

—A “reality receipt” appears: “RESOLVED.” Money releases.

—A guarantee pool absorbs a failure: “Outcome not achieved. Refund issued.” The specialist is still treated fairly—partial comp for effort, clear terms.

—A community board lists verified providers: reputation built on outcomes, not ads.

—People work less on screens, more in reality: walking while agents coordinate, meeting in person while logistics hum in the background.

The economy doesn’t collapse. It re-prices.


63. INT. KINGDOM HQ — CELESTE’S OFFICE — DAY

Celeste watches news on her monitor.

Not hype pieces—quiet stories:
“Outcome-based systems reduce admin burden in clinics.”
“Verified responsibility markets emerge.”

A staffer enters.

                         STAFFER
             They’re calling it… a bridge economy.

Celeste’s eyes narrow. Not anger—thoughtfulness.

                         CELESTE
             And Meaning Mode?

                         STAFFER
             Adoption is still strong.
             But… churn is creeping.
             People say it feels… sticky.

Celeste exhales—she knew.

                         CELESTE
             Bring me a proposal.
             Something with verification.
             Guarantees.
             Human sign-off.

The staffer hesitates.

                         STAFFER
             Like them?

Celeste looks out the window at the city.

                         CELESTE
             Like reality.

A beat.

                         CELESTE (CONT’D)
             We don’t get to stop the crossing.
             We only get to reduce harm.

The staffer nods.

Celeste sits down. The rock is still on her back.

She lifts it again.


64. EXT. QUIET CAFE PATIO — LATE AFTERNOON

Mara sits alone at an outdoor table.

No laptop. No phone.

A notebook. A pen.

She watches people walk by, actually seeing them.

Lens is present—silent.

A SERVER drops off tea.

                         SERVER
             Anything else?

Mara smiles.

                         MARA
             No.
             This is… enough.

The server walks away.

Mara writes one line in her notebook:

“PRACTICE IS THE PRODUCT.”

She closes the notebook.

She sits with her tea, the world alive around her.

For the first time, she isn’t performing progress.

She is living it.


65. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — EVENING

The door opens.

Elias steps inside, carrying a small bag of groceries.

He freezes when he sees Mara at the workbench.

Mara stands slowly. No defensiveness.

Elias studies her face—looking for the kingdom.

He doesn’t see it.

He exhales.

                         ELIAS
             You turned it off.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             I turned it down.
             I turned myself up.

Elias’s eyes glisten—relief.

He sets the groceries down.

A long beat where neither knows what to say.

Then Mara says the truth.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             I’m sorry.

Elias nods. He doesn’t make her beg.

                         ELIAS
             I know.

Mara gestures to the Lens device.

                         MARA
             It’s quieter now.

Elias approaches, looks at it like you look at a storm that might become weather.

                         ELIAS
             And you?

Mara breathes. Steady.

                         MARA
             I’m… here.

Elias smiles—small, real.

                         ELIAS
             Good.

They start putting groceries away together.

No montage. Just mundane intimacy.


66. EXT. CITY ROOFTOP OVERLOOK — DAWN

Mara and Elias stand on the same rooftop as Act I.

The city below still glows, but less oppressive now—like it’s one layer, not the whole world.

Mara’s phone is off.

Elias’s hands are in his pockets.

A pale sunrise.

Lens is not visible.

But the air feels aligned, quieter.

Mara takes a full breath.

Elias notices.

                         ELIAS
             You okay?

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             I don’t know where this ends.

Elias smiles gently.

                         ELIAS
             Me neither.

Mara looks toward the horizon.

                         MARA
             But I know what I’m not doing anymore.

Elias waits.

Mara says it, soft but absolute.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             I’m not looking back.

A beat.

Lens speaks—only one word, like a bell.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Intent?

Mara smiles—not at the device, at the sky.

                         MARA
             Yes.

She takes Elias’s hand. Simple. Human.

They stand as the sun rises.

Not victory.

Crossing.

FADE OUT.

END OF ACT III
