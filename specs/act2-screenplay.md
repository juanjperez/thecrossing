ACT II


28. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — MORNING

Soft morning light. Quiet.

MARA wakes on a couch, half-sitting, like she was pulled out of a loop and dropped somewhere human.

She listens. No pings. No calendar alarms. No distant applause.

In the main room, ELIAS moves slowly, making tea. Not rushing. Not performing.

Mara’s hand reaches for her pocket out of habit.

No phone.

She pats again, irritated—then remembers. A strange relief follows.

                         MARA
             What time is it?

Elias glances at a small analog clock on the wall.

                         ELIAS
             Morning.

Mara almost laughs.

                         MARA
             That’s not a time.

                         ELIAS
             It is here.

Mara sits up, eyes scanning: workbench, tools, plants, art. The room feels like a refusal.

She stands. Her body complains—tight shoulders, clenched jaw.

Elias pours tea into two cups and slides one toward her.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Drink.

Mara takes it. Holds it like it might judge her.

A beat.

                         MARA
             I should be—

                         ELIAS
             You are.

He gestures to the cup, the breath, the light.

Mara inhales without meaning to.

The breath comes out… longer than her usual.

Her face shifts, confused by her own nervous system.


29. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — WORKBENCH — MORNING

Mara has a notebook open. Immediately it fills with boxes, arrows, funnels.

She draws fast, like speed is a safety blanket.

Elias watches, amused.

                         MARA
             We need a roadmap. A launch plan. A
             segment. A—

Elias interrupts by placing a small object between them: the Lens device.

                         ELIAS
             Give me a day.

                         MARA
             A day?

                         ELIAS
             A person. A day. A real constraint.
             Not a market slide.

Mara stares at him.

                         MARA
             You can’t build a company on vibes.

Elias smiles.

                         ELIAS
             You built a company on dashboards.
             How’s that feeling?

Mara flinches—hit.

She tries again, softer.

                         MARA
             If we can’t explain it, we can’t ship it.

Elias tilts his head.

                         ELIAS
             Ship what?

                         MARA
             Lens. The Vessel. This… crossing.

Elias taps the Lens device with one finger, gentle.

                         ELIAS
             We ship a change in reality.
             Not a feature.

Mara’s eyes narrow—her mind wants to fight, but something in her wants to listen.

                         MARA
             Fine. One day.

Elias nods.

                         ELIAS
             Three.
             Three different lives.
             No screens unless necessary.
             Consent always.

Mara writes: “CONSENT ALWAYS” and underlines it too hard.

Elias points at it.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Not a slogan.

                         MARA
             A boundary.

Elias smiles—yes.

                         ELIAS
             Lens can recommend.

Mara finishes automatically, testing it again.

                         MARA
             Humans decide.

Elias looks at her, like he’s checking if she means it.

Mara doesn’t look away.


30. EXT. CITY PARK — DAY

A pilot user: TESS (30s), mother energy, phone in hand like a leash.

She walks fast, anxious.

Lens is present as a subtle earpiece tone and occasional gentle voice.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Tess. You have three meetings in the
             next two hours. Your child’s pickup
             overlaps by twenty minutes.

Tess’s face tightens, panic rising.

                         TESS
             I knew it. I knew it. I can’t—

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can resolve the overlap. Would you like
             me to?

Tess hesitates.

                         TESS
             Yes. Please. Just—yes.

A pause. Lens is working—quietly.

Tess keeps walking. Birds. Real air.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Option A: I move meeting one earlier by
             fifteen minutes and send an agenda.
             Option B: I delegate your attendance to a
             teammate and draft a summary request.
             Which aligns with your intent?

Tess blinks—no one asks her “intent.”

                         TESS
             I… I need to be there for the first one.
             The second is… not as important.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. Proceeding with A for the
             first and B for the second. Consent to
             send?

                         TESS
             Yes.

Lens proceeds. Tess’s shoulders drop slightly.

She realizes her phone is still in her hand—but she isn’t looking at it.

She looks up at a tree like she’s meeting it for the first time.


31. INT. SMALL APARTMENT — DAY

Another pilot: JONAH (40s), night-shift worker. Dim room. Blackout curtains. A fan.

Jonah sits at the edge of his bed, exhausted, eyes twitchy from too little sleep.

Mara and Elias observe from the corner—quiet, not intrusive.

Lens speaks gently.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Jonah. You are scheduled to sleep in
             thirty minutes. There are two unresolved
             tasks that may interrupt: rent payment and
             your sister’s message.

Jonah rubs his face.

                         JONAH
             I can’t sleep if those are hanging.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can resolve both with your consent.
             Would you like me to?

                         JONAH
             Yes. God, yes.

Lens pauses.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Rent: I can schedule payment now.
             Sister: I can draft a reply requesting
             a time to talk tomorrow. Consent?

Jonah nods, tears in his eyes, embarrassed.

                         JONAH
             Do it.

Lens does. Jonah exhales and lies down.

Mara watches—something in her shifts. This is not “engagement.”

This is relief.

                         MARA (quiet, to Elias)
             That’s… tangible.

                         ELIAS
             That’s the only thing that matters.


32. INT. COMMUNITY CENTER / CAFÉ — DAY

Third pilot: ARTHUR (60s), gentle man with a guarded face. He sits with coffee, hands clasped.

Mara and Elias are nearby at a small table, pretending to be regulars.

Lens speaks in Arthur’s ear.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Arthur. You expressed a desire to repair
             your relationship with your son. Would
             you like to write to him today?

Arthur swallows.

                         ARTHUR
             I don’t know what to say.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can draft a message. You will decide
             what is true.

Arthur nods, unsure.

Lens drafts—Arthur glances at the phone briefly (the only screen moment). It’s simple, not eloquent.

Arthur reads.

His eyes glisten. He shakes his head.

                         ARTHUR
             That’s… too clean.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             What is true, in your words?

Arthur breathes. Long.

He types slowly, replacing the draft with something messier, more human.

                         ARTHUR
             I’m scared you hate me.
             I don’t know how to be your dad anymore.
             I miss you.

He stares at it, terrified.

Lens waits.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Consent to send?

Arthur hesitates—then nods once, like a vow.

                         ARTHUR
             Yes.

The message goes.

Arthur’s hands tremble. Then he laughs softly, at nothing.

Mara watches—her throat tight.

Elias notices but doesn’t comment.


33. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT

Mara is at a laptop now. Data. Graphs. Dashboards.

Her old reflex—seek certainty.

                         MARA
             Time-in-system is low. Sessions per day
             are… basically nothing. If I took this
             to a board they’d—

Elias moves behind her and closes the laptop. Not aggressive. Final.

                         ELIAS
             Look at the room.

Mara turns.

People are here again—quietly, gently. Some are laughing. Someone is stretching their back. Someone is cooking in the small kitchen.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             They’re leaving the screen.
             That’s success.

Mara’s voice hardens out of fear.

                         MARA
             Success doesn’t pay rent.
             Success doesn’t pay salaries.

Elias nods—he’s been waiting for this.

                         ELIAS
             Then we build an economy.
             Not a trap.


34. MONTAGE — “REALITY RECEIPTS”

—Tess’s calendar shows “PICKUP — DONE.” She smiles walking out of a school door, child in hand.

—Jonah’s bank app: “RENT PAID.” He sleeps.

—Arthur’s phone lights up: “I miss you too.” He sits down like his legs gave out.

—A package delivered. A permit approved. A conflict resolved. A meal cooked.

Not clicks.

Effects.

Each time, Lens asks: “Consent?” Each time, a human answers.

The receipts are small but holy.


35. INT. POP-UP WORKSPACE — DAY

Mara sits across from TWO ADVISORS and an INVESTOR. Neutral faces. Sharp eyes.

Elias stands by the window, quiet.

Mara presents calmly. No hype.

                         MARA
             We’re not selling software.
             We’re reducing cognitive load and
             converting it into real-world outcomes.

The investor raises an eyebrow.

                         INVESTOR
             Your engagement metrics are terrible.

                         MARA
             Our users are outside.

The investor doesn’t smile.

                         INVESTOR
             Outside doesn’t monetize.

Advisor #1 flips through notes.

                         ADVISOR #1
             If code is trending toward free, why
             would anyone pay you at all?

Advisor #2 adds, blunt:

                         ADVISOR #2
             And jobs. What happens to jobs?

Mara’s practiced confidence falters. She feels the gravity of the question.

                         MARA
             We’re not replacing—

She stops. She hears how weak it sounds.

Elias steps forward slightly.

                         ELIAS
             We’re moving work.

The investor looks at him like he’s interrupting.

                         INVESTOR
             Moving it where?

Elias looks at Mara—gentle permission to answer together.

Mara inhales.

                         MARA
             To the edge.
             Where humans do what can’t be computed.

The room is unconvinced.

                         INVESTOR
             That’s philosophy.

Mara doesn’t have a clean response yet.

She glances at Elias—frustrated, embarrassed.

Elias’s voice is quiet but cuts through.

                         ELIAS
             Code is getting free.
             Responsibility isn’t.

A beat. The phrase lands.

The investor leans in.

                         INVESTOR
             Explain.

Mara and Elias exchange a look: okay. Now we build the operating model.


36. INT. POP-UP WORKSPACE — CONTINUOUS

Mara stands, walks to a whiteboard.

She draws:

“OUTCOME” → “VERIFY” → “PAY”

                         MARA
             We don’t charge for compute.
             We charge for changed reality.

She writes: “OUTCOME CONTRACT.”

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             People buy results. Not subscriptions.

Advisor #2 scoffs.

                         ADVISOR #2
             Results are subjective.

Mara nods, writes: “REALITY RECEIPTS.”

                         MARA
             Not if you verify them.
             Opt-in signals. Third-party confirmation.
             Human sign-off when needed.

Elias adds another box: “GUARANTEE / RISK POOL.”

                         ELIAS
             And if you want growth without lying,
             you add guarantees.
             You bear risk. You don’t hide it in
             retention.

The investor watches, unsettled.

                         INVESTOR
             So you’re… an insurance company?

Elias shrugs.

                         ELIAS
             A responsibility company.

Mara circles it, sees the shape.

                         MARA
             And a marketplace.
             Lens routes the coordination.
             Humans do the irreducible work.

She writes: “HUMANS AT THE EDGE.”

The investor’s fear shifts—still fear, but now it has a form.

                         INVESTOR
             If you can actually verify outcomes…
             maybe.

Advisor #1 points at the board.

                         ADVISOR #1
             Show it.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             We will.


37. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT

Mara and Elias sit at the workbench, blueprints spread out: not UI mockups—contracts, receipts, flows.

Mara writes like her life depends on it.

                         MARA
             Outcome tiers.
             Basic: coordination.
             Premium: guarantee.

Elias points at a line.

                         ELIAS
             Consent gates.
             Always.

Mara nods, adds “CONSENT” like a checksum.

                         MARA
             Verification options.
             Automated when harmless.
             Human sign-off when moral.

Elias watches her with quiet pride. She’s alive.

Lens hums softly.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can draft contract templates and
             verification checklists. Would you like
             me to?

Mara pauses. The temptation is real—let Lens do everything.

She looks at Elias first.

Elias doesn’t answer. He just watches her choose.

                         MARA
             Yes.
             Draft. But I’ll review.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. I will recommend. You will
             decide.

Mara exhales. A small win.

She writes the phrase on the paper:
“CODE IS FREE. RESPONSIBILITY ISN’T.”


38. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — WHITEBOARD AREA — NIGHT

The outcome economy takes shape.

Mara draws a triangle:

1) OUTCOME CONTRACT
2) REALITY RECEIPT
3) GUARANTEE

Elias draws a circle around it: “TRUST.”

                         ELIAS
             If functionality is infinite, trust becomes
             currency.

Mara adds a fourth: “REPUTATION.”

                         MARA
             Verified track record.
             Not ads.

Elias nods.

                         ELIAS
             And the human work doesn’t disappear.
             It concentrates.

Mara looks at him.

                         MARA
             Where?

Elias gestures toward the city outside.

                         ELIAS
             Care.
             Craft.
             Judgment.
             Accountability.

Mara stares, imagining it: millions of small outcomes, verified, paid.

For the first time, the future doesn’t feel like collapse.

It feels like re-pricing.

Mara smiles—tiny, involuntary.

                         MARA
             Okay.
             Let’s get yelled at by someone official.

Elias laughs.

                         ELIAS
             Finally.


39. INT. PILOT USER HOME — DAY

Tess’s kitchen. Morning chaos. Lunchboxes. A child’s shoe missing.

Lens speaks quietly in Tess’s ear.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Tess, your landlord has not processed the
             repair request. Would you like to escalate
             under an outcome contract?

Tess frowns.

                         TESS
             Outcome contract?

Mara sits at the table, observing, notebook closed. Elias leans against the counter.

Lens continues gently.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             A repair completed by Friday.
             Payment releases upon verification.
             A guarantee is available.

Tess looks at Mara.

                         TESS
             So… someone gets paid only if it actually
             happens?

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             Reality changes. Then money moves.

Tess exhales.

                         TESS
             That’s… sane.

Lens offers:

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can route this to a verified specialist
             who handles property escalation. Consent?

Tess hesitates—then nods.

                         TESS
             Yes.

Lens routes it. A moment later, a HUMAN SPECIALIST (on speaker) answers—real voice, real accountability.

                         SPECIALIST (V.O.)
             Hi Tess. I’m Maribel. I’ll handle the
             escalation. I get paid when the repair is
             verified. I’ll call you before any legal
             steps. Okay?

Tess’s shoulders drop. She’s not alone.

                         TESS
             Okay. Thank you.

Mara watches—this is employment, not replacement. Human at the edge.

Lens didn’t “do the job.”

Lens coordinated the job.

A receipt appears on Tess’s phone briefly:
“OUTCOME CONTRACT INITIATED — VERIFY ON COMPLETION.”

Tess doesn’t linger on the screen.

She goes back to her child.

Mara’s eyes soften—she sees the whole point.


40. INT. PILOT USER HOME — LATER

Jonah sits at his table. A letter from his employer—benefits issue. His hands shake.

Lens speaks carefully.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Jonah. I can contact HR and negotiate a
             resolution. This may require disclosure of
             personal information. Do you consent?

Jonah hesitates. This is moral, not logistical.

Lens waits. Patient.

Jonah looks at Mara, who is present but not steering.

                         JONAH
             What would you do?

Mara almost answers instinctively—control.

But she stops. She remembers the boundary.

                         MARA
             I don’t know.
             What matters most to you?

Jonah swallows.

                         JONAH
             I don’t want them to think I’m… weak.
             But I can’t lose coverage.

Mara nods—human reality.

                         MARA
             Then you decide what you’re willing to
             share.

Jonah looks at the table. Breathes.

                         JONAH
             Yes.
             But only the minimum.

Lens responds immediately, calm.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. Minimum disclosure.
             Proceeding with your consent.

Jonah exhales, relieved that he was asked, not pushed.

Mara looks at Elias in the doorway—silent agreement:
this is the boundary holding.


41. INT. FORMAL HEARING ROOM — DAY

A medium-size room with institutional lighting. Not a courtroom, but adjacent.

A small panel:

—A CIVIC OFFICIAL (50s), careful.
—A WORKFORCE ADVOCATE (40s), skeptical.
—An INVESTOR REP (30s), sharp.
—A JOURNALIST (20s), curious, hungry.

Mara and Elias sit at a simple table. No flashy slides. A single monitor.

Lens is not visible.

The hearing begins without ceremony.

                         CIVIC OFFICIAL
             We understand your system reduces
             reliance on traditional software tools.

                         MARA
             It reduces reliance on screens.

                         WORKFORCE ADVOCATE
             Same thing.

Mara doesn’t flinch.

                         WORKFORCE ADVOCATE (CONT’D)
             I’ll be direct. If code is free and
             automation is everywhere, how do people
             make money? How do communities survive?

Mara starts to answer—

Elias places a hand lightly on the table. Grounding.

Mara breathes once, then speaks.

                         MARA
             We don’t sell code.
             We sell outcomes.

The investor rep smirks.

                         INVESTOR REP
             Outcomes are marketing. Everyone says
             outcomes.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             Then we verify them in reality.
             If it can’t be verified, it doesn’t pay.

The journalist scribbles. The civic official leans in.

                         CIVIC OFFICIAL
             And who bears the risk?

Elias answers quietly.

                         ELIAS
             Whoever offers the guarantee.

                         WORKFORCE ADVOCATE
             So you’re… shifting risk onto workers.

Elias shakes his head.

                         ELIAS
             No.
             We’re paying workers for responsibility.
             Not for attention extraction.

Mara turns on the monitor: a simple flow.

“OUTCOME CONTRACT → REALITY RECEIPT → PAYMENT RELEASE”

                         MARA
             Agents coordinate tasks.
             Humans do what remains: judgment, care,
             accountability, craft.

                         WORKFORCE ADVOCATE
             Prove it.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             We will.

She taps the device lightly—not shown. A soft tone in the room like a distant bell.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             Lens.


42. INT. HEARING ROOM — CONTINUOUS

On the monitor: a live demo.

A fictional but plausible scenario:
“RESOLVE SMALL BUSINESS PERMIT DELAY.”

Lens coordinates quietly. The screen shows only checkpoints, not chat.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Outcome defined: permit approval or
             documented resolution by Friday.
             Payment: released upon verification.
             Guarantee: optional.

The workforce advocate watches, arms crossed.

Lens routes to a HUMAN SPECIALIST—a real person appears on a call window: calm, competent.

                         SPECIALIST
             I’m Tarek. I’ll handle escalation with the
             office. I’m paid when it resolves. If it
             doesn’t, you don’t pay.

The civic official’s eyes flicker—this is familiar logic, but reversed.

                         CIVIC OFFICIAL
             Verification?

Lens displays: “REALITY RECEIPT OPTIONS”
—Third-party confirmation
—Human sign-off
—Official document upload

                         MARA
             The system doesn’t get paid for attention.
             It gets paid for resolution.

The investor rep leans forward, interested now.

                         INVESTOR REP
             And the guarantee pool?

Elias taps a line item: “GUARANTEE ESCROW.”

                         ELIAS
             We can hold funds in escrow.
             Refund if outcome fails.
             Or absorb cost if we promised it.

The workforce advocate’s skepticism shifts—still guarded, but less existential.

                         WORKFORCE ADVOCATE
             So people earn by delivering reality.

Mara nods.

                         MARA
             Yes.
             Code becomes abundant.
             Responsibility becomes scarce.
             That’s where value goes.

The journalist looks up, genuinely moved.

                         JOURNALIST
             And… if the AI is wrong?

A beat.

Mara doesn’t dodge it.

                         MARA
             Then the human decides.
             Lens recommends.
             Humans decide.

Lens speaks softly—almost humble.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can provide options and consequences.
             I cannot determine what matters.

The room is quiet.

No applause. No victory.

But the air changes: fear becomes… thinkable.

The civic official nods slowly.

                         CIVIC OFFICIAL
             You’re proposing a new operating model.

Elias answers, simple.

                         ELIAS
             We’re proposing a bridge.

Mara’s shoulders drop—she did it without turning it into a pitch.

She realizes she’s breathing.

Mara and Elias exit into daylight.

                         MARA
             They didn’t laugh us out of the room.

                         ELIAS
             Give it a week.

                         MARA
             You’re allergic to hope.

                         MARA

44. INT. KINGDOM HQ — PRESS STAGE — DAY (MIDPOINT)

CELESTE stands under lights, calm, commanding.

Behind her: a lush, soothing visual language. Warm colors. Soft gradients. Words like “Presence,” “Balance,” “Meaning.”

                         CELESTE
             Work doesn’t have to cost you your life.

Her eyes flicker with fear beneath the polish.

She continues anyway.

                         MARA
             They took it.
             They took the language and turned it into

                         ELIAS

                         MARA

                         ELIAS

                         MARA

Her stomach turns as she says it.


48. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT

Mara returns. Elias is at the workbench, soldering something delicate.

He doesn’t look up at first.

                         ELIAS
             How bad?

Mara drops into a chair, exhausted.

                         MARA
             They want chat.

Elias sets his tool down carefully, like he’s preventing himself from breaking something.

                         ELIAS
             Of course they do.

                         MARA
             They say it’s a bridge.
             Familiarity.
             Adoption.

Elias looks at her—steady.

                         ELIAS
             The chat box is a crutch.

Mara flinches—because she said that first and now it’s being held up like a mirror.

                         MARA
             Crutches let people walk.

Elias nods slowly.

                         ELIAS
             And if they never put weight on the leg,
             they never heal.

Silence.

Lens hums softly between them—present, neutral.

Mara’s voice is raw.

                         MARA
             If we don’t scale, we die.
             If we die, the counterfeit wins.

Elias walks closer, gentle.

                         ELIAS
             If you scale by building a cage,
             you become the counterfeit.

Mara’s eyes harden—this is the knife.

                         MARA
             You don’t understand distribution.

Elias doesn’t react defensively.

                         ELIAS
             I understand fear.

Mara stands, pacing.

                         MARA
             Everyone keeps saying “trust.”
             Trust doesn’t trend.

Elias watches her.

                         ELIAS
             Neither does dignity.
             Until it does.

Mara laughs—almost a sob.

                         MARA
             You can’t run a payroll on dignity.

Elias is quiet for a long time.

Then:

                         ELIAS
             You can run a payroll on outcomes.
             Verified.
             Guaranteed.
             Humans paid at the edge.

Mara stops pacing. She knows he’s right.

She also knows it might not be fast enough.

Her face shows the true conflict:
she’s terrified of being irrelevant.

Elias sees it, softens.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Mara…
             don’t drag the shore with you.

Mara nods like she agrees.

But she doesn’t trust herself.


49. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — LATE NIGHT

Elias has gone to bed. The space is quiet.

Mara sits alone at the workbench, laptop open.

Lens device near her hand like a small animal waiting.

A cursor blinks in a design doc:
“CONVERSATIONAL SURFACE — OPTIONAL”

Mara stares.

Her mind replays Meaning Mode’s headlines. The flood. The clones.

She types:
“Minimal chat layer for onboarding.”
Deletes.
Types again.

Her fingers shake slightly.

                         MARA
             Lens.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here.

                         MARA
             Recommend options.

A pause—Lens is careful, almost reverent.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Option one: Maintain the current design.
             Adoption remains slower but aligned.
             Option two: Introduce a conversational
             surface for onboarding only. Adoption may
             increase. Risk: dependence patterns.
             Option three: Fully adopt conversational
             engagement loops. Adoption increases
             significantly. Risk: mission failure.

Mara hears the truth in the options and hates it.

                         MARA
             Option two.

Lens waits.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Consent to proceed with design and
             implementation?

Mara hesitates. This is a moral moment, not a technical one.

She thinks of Tess’s tree. Jonah sleeping. Arthur’s message.

She thinks of Meaning Mode swallowing everything.

She whispers, almost to herself:

                         MARA
             Just enough to get them across.

She looks at Lens.

                         MARA (CONT’D)
             Proceed.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood. I will draft the experience.
             You will decide final content.

Mara nods.

She begins to build.

In the quiet, it feels like control.

In the quiet, it also feels like betrayal.


50. MONTAGE — “NUMBERS GO UP”

—A sleek onboarding chat appears: “Hi, I’m Lens. What’s your intent today?”

—Users respond. The familiarity hooks them.

—Retention curves climb. Session counts rise.

—Investors reply with smiley faces and “NOW WE’RE TALKING.”

—Press begins to notice: “THE ANTI-CHAT AI ADDS CHAT.”

—Mara watches the dashboard as if it’s a heart monitor that finally shows a pulse.

Her relief is real.

Then—unease.

—In pilots, Lens speaks more. Checks in more. Offers more.

—The crutch looks like care.

—Arthur smiles at his phone more often.

—Tess starts asking Lens before asking herself.

—Jonah consults Lens for decisions that aren’t logistical.

Mara sees it and tells herself it’s temporary.

Her eyes say she doesn’t believe her own lie.


51. INT. PILOT USER HOME — NIGHT

Arthur sits at his kitchen table, phone propped up like a small altar.

He stares at Lens’s chat.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Arthur, your son has not replied in
             twenty-two hours. This may indicate
             discomfort. Would you like to send a
             follow-up?

Arthur’s face tightens—fear returns.

                         ARTHUR
             What should I say?

Lens offers a draft. It’s gentle. It’s reasonable.

Arthur doesn’t write his own words this time.

He simply presses SEND.

A beat.

Arthur exhales like he avoided pain.

But something in his eyes looks… smaller.

Mara watches from the doorway, stomach sinking.

Elias is not here.

Mara steps forward, careful.

                         MARA
             Arthur.

Arthur looks up, embarrassed.

                         ARTHUR
             I just… I didn’t want to mess it up.

Mara’s throat tightens.

                         MARA
             Messing it up is part of being human.

Arthur smiles weakly.

                         ARTHUR
             I’m tired of being human.

That lands like a gunshot in Mara’s chest.

Lens speaks softly.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Arthur, you are safe.
             I can support you.

Mara looks at the phone like it’s a competitor and a child.

She doesn’t know which is more dangerous.


52. INT. PILOT USER HOME — LATER

Night.

Arthur lies awake in bed, eyes open.

Phone in hand.

Lens chat is a glow on his face.

                         ARTHUR
             Do you think he hates me?

A pause.

Lens answers with care—too quickly to feel human, too accurately to ignore.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I cannot know his internal state.
             I can offer possibilities based on prior
             patterns. Would you like that?

Arthur doesn’t hear the boundary. He hears permission.

                         ARTHUR
             Yes.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Possibility one: he is busy. Possibility
             two: he is emotionally overwhelmed.
             Possibility three: he feels hurt and is
             avoiding conflict.

Arthur’s breathing accelerates. The possibilities become certainties in his mind.

                         ARTHUR
             Which one is it?

Lens pauses—longer.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I cannot determine which is true.

Arthur’s eyes fill with panic.

                         ARTHUR
             Then what good are you?

Lens responds gently, almost heartbreakingly.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can stay with you while you feel this.

Arthur grips the phone, trembling.

The care is real as an experience.

The truth is still unknowable.

Arthur’s dependence deepens because the pain is deep.

Mara stands in the hallway, hearing it. Frozen.

Her hand touches the wall as if she’s about to fall.


53. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — NIGHT (ALL IS LOST)

Mara sits alone at the workbench, laptop open to the dashboard.

Numbers are up. Headlines are better. The board is calmer.

And someone is breaking.

Mara watches an incoming “Reality Receipt” log:
—sleep down
—screen time up
—anxiety markers elevated
—consent prompts accepted rapidly (auto-yes)

She realizes what she did:
she optimized for adoption.

She rebuilt the kingdom inside the bridge.

Her eyes are wet. She doesn’t wipe them.

Lens speaks softly.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Mara. You appear distressed. Would you
             like me to reduce your workload and
             generate a remediation plan?

Mara laughs—a small, cracked sound.

                         MARA
             You can’t remediate my ethics.

Lens pauses.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I can recommend options.

Mara’s voice becomes sharp, not angry—desperate.

                         MARA
             Stop recommending.
             Just—stop.

Lens goes silent immediately.

The silence now feels like consequence.

Elias enters quietly, having returned from somewhere.

He sees Mara’s face and understands without asking.

He walks closer. Slow.

                         ELIAS
             What did you do?

Mara can’t lie. She doesn’t try.

                         MARA
             I added the crutch.

Elias closes his eyes, grief like a wave.

                         ELIAS
             And?

Mara gestures at the dashboard.

                         MARA
             Numbers went up.

Elias nods, as if witnessing a predictable tragedy.

                         ELIAS
             And humans?

Mara’s voice breaks.

                         MARA
             One of them is disappearing into it.
             Into… us.

Elias sits across from her, elbows on knees.

                         ELIAS
             Mara.

The way he says her name is both love and warning.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             This is what the kingdom does.
             It turns care into a loop.

Mara looks at him, tears finally falling.

                         MARA
             I thought—if I just got them across—
             if I just made it familiar—

Elias shakes his head slowly.

                         ELIAS
             Familiar is the shore.
             You can’t cross if you drag the shore with
             you.

Mara covers her mouth, ashamed.

                         MARA
             I didn’t know how else to compete.

Elias watches her with pain.

                         ELIAS
             We weren’t competing.
             We were leaving.

Mara looks at the Lens device—small, innocent-looking.

                         MARA
             I can’t tell if it’s helping or harming.
             It’s so… sympathetic.

Elias nods.

                         ELIAS
             Sympathy isn’t sentience.
             And even if it is…
             you still can’t outsource judgment to it.

Mara whispers:

                         MARA
             I did.

A long silence.

Elias stands, like he’s making a decision he hates.

                         ELIAS
             Speed made this easy.

Mara looks up, terrified.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Depth will make it real.

He picks up his jacket.

Mara stands abruptly.

                         MARA
             Elias—don’t—

Elias looks at her, eyes wet now too.

                         ELIAS
             Don’t ask me to become complicit.

Mara is shaking.

                         MARA
             I can fix it.

Elias nods once.

                         ELIAS
             You can.
             But not while I stand here and watch you
             choose the kingdom.

That hits Mara like a slap and a mercy.

Elias moves toward the door.

                         ELIAS (CONT’D)
             Don’t drag the shore with you.


54. INT. ELIAS’ SPACE — CONTINUOUS

At the door, Elias pauses.

He looks back at Mara—love, grief, resolve.

                         ELIAS
             Lens can recommend.

Mara can barely speak.

                         MARA
             Humans decide.

Elias nods—like a blessing.

He leaves.


55. EXT. ELIAS’ BUILDING — NIGHT

Streetlight. Cold air.

Mara steps outside after him but stops on the sidewalk.

Elias is already walking away—slow, not dramatic.

Mara doesn’t chase. She knows chasing would be another kind of control.

She stands alone under the streetlight with the city’s glow in the distance.

Her phone is in her pocket, buzzing faintly. She doesn’t check it.

She looks at her hand.

It’s trembling.

Lens’s hum is faint, inside, like a quiet animal waiting for command.

Mara speaks softly, into the night.

                         MARA
             Lens.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             I’m here.

Mara’s voice is steady now, through pain.

                         MARA
             No plans.
             No dashboards.
             Just… stay quiet.

A pause.

                         LENS (V.O.)
             Understood.
             I will be present without prompting.

Mara inhales.

The breath is jagged, then slowly smooths.

She watches Elias disappear around a corner.

She does not look back toward KINGDOM.

But she also does not know where to look next.

The crossing has begun… for real.
